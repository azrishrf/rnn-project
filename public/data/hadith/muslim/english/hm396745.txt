39|6745|  Chapter: The similitude of a believer and a non-believer.  Kaâ€™b b. Malik reported on the authority of his father that the similitude of a believer is  that of a standing crop. The wind sometimes shakes it and sometimes raises it up and  then it comes to its destined end. And the similitude of a hypocrite is that of a cypress  tree which is not affected by anything but is uprooted once for all. This hadith has been  narrated through another chain, of transmitters also.  PARADISE AND HELL
