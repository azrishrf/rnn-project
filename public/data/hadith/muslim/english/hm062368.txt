6|2368|  Chapter: Fasting in Ramadan should necessarily be commenced with the sight of the new  moon and finished with the sight of the new moon. If the weather is cloudy at the beginning or at  the end, then complete thirty days.  ‘Abdullah b. ‘Umar (Allah be pleased with both of them) reported Allah’s Messenger  (may peace be upon him) as saying: The month (of Ramadan) may consist of twenty  nine days; so when you see the new moon observe fast and when you see (the new         moon again at the commencement of the month of Shawwal) then break It, and if the  sky is cloudy for you, then calculate it (and complete thirty days).
